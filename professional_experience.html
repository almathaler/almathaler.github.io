Professional Experience
As a freshman I conducted research with Capra research group on Calyx. Calyx is a compiler infrastructure for languages that target hardware accelerators. Its control language is an alternative to other HDLs. In particular I helped work on the interpreter, which is written in Rust.

As a sophomore I interned at Meta, working on the HackC team. HackC is the compiler for Meta's Hack language, which is an alternative to PHP. In particular, I wrote an assembler that helps verify that the compiled output of a Hack program is as expected. This project was also in Rust.

As a junior I interned at Jane Street. My project was to extend an OCaml PPX to support a new data type. I will be returning to Jane Street in the fall of 2024 to start my career as a Software Engineer.
